SUMMARY OF KEY POINTS

•HAMILTONIAN GRAPHS:

o HAMILTONIAN PATH: VISITS EACH VERTEX EXACTLY ONCE.
ITS EXISTENCE DEPENDS ON THE GRAPH’S STRUCTURE;
THERE IS NO SIMPLE RULE TO DETERMINE IT.

o HAMILTONIAN CIRCUIT: A CLOSED LOOP THAT VISITS EACH VERTEX ONCE.
LIKE HAMILTONIAN PATHS, DETERMINING A HAMILTONIAN CIRCUIT IS COMPUTATIONALLY CHALLENGING.

ADVANCED CONCEPTS IN GRAPH THEORY

• WEIGHTED GRAPHS: EACH EDGE HAS A WEIGHT, OFTEN USED IN ROUTE OPTIMIZATION TO REPRESENT DISTANCES, COSTS, OR CAPACITIES.

• PLANAR GRAPHS: GRAPHS THAT CAN BE DRAWN ON A PLANE WITHOUT ANY EDGES CROSSING, USEFUL IN GEOGRAPHICAL MAPPING AND CIRCUIT DESIGN.

• CONNECTIVITY: MEASURES A GRAPH'S RESILIENCE TO EDGE OR VERTEX REMOVAL. A GRAPH IS STRONGLY CONNECTED IF THERE’S A PATH BETWEEN EVERY PAIR OF VERTICES IN A DIRECTED GRAPH, CRITICAL IN NETWORK RELIABILITY
