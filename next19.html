EULERIAN AND HAMILTONIAN PROPERTIES IN DIRECTED GRAPHS

• EULERIAN PATH AND CIRCUIT:

o EULERIAN PATH: EXISTS IF ONE VERTEX HAS ONE EXTRA OUTGOING EDGE, ANOTHER ONE EXTRA INCOMING, AND ALL OTHERS ARE BALANCED.
o EULERIAN CIRCUIT: REQUIRES EQUAL INCOMING AND OUTGOING EDGES FOR ALL VERTICES, WITH STRONG CONNECTIVITY.

• HAMILTONIAN PATH AND CIRCUIT:

o HAMILTONIAN PATH: VISITS EACH VERTEX ONCE; DEPENDS ON STRUCTURE, NOT EASILY DETERMINED.
o HAMILTONIAN CIRCUIT: FORMS A LOOP VISITING EACH VERTEX ONCE; COMPLEX TO IDENTIFY DUE TO NP-COMPLETENESS.
