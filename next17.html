DIRECTED GRAPH

A DIRECTED GRAPH (OR DIGRAPH) IS A TYPE OF GRAPH WHERE EACH EDGE HAS A SPECIFIC DIRECTION.
IN A DIRECTED GRAPH, AN EDGE IS REPRESENTED AS AN ORDERED PAIR OF VERTICES, INDICATING A ONE-WAY CONNECTION FROM THE STARTING VERTEX (TAIL) TO THE ENDING VERTEX (HEAD).
THIS DIRECTIONALITY CONTRASTS WITH UNDIRECTED GRAPHS, WHERE EDGES HAVE NO INHERENT DIRECTION, AND CONNECTIONS ARE TWO-WAY.
