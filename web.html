I. Introduction to Graph Theory

Definition of Graphs:

Graph Theory is a branch of mathematics that studies graphs, which are structures used to model pairwise relationships between objects.
It has applications across many disciplines, including computer science, biology, social networks, and transport systems, among others.
A graph is a collection of objects called vertices (or nodes) and the connections between them, called edges (or arcs).
Graph theory provides a framework for analyzing and solving problems related to networks, connectivity, flow, and structure.
