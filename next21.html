SUMMARY OF KEY POINTS

•DIRECTED GRAPHS: THESE GRAPHS HAVE EDGES WITH SPECIFIC DIRECTIONS, IDEAL FOR MODELING RELATIONSHIPS LIKE TRAFFIC FLOW, FLIGHT PATHS, AND SOCIAL NETWORKS.

EULERIAN GRAPHS:

• EULERIAN PATH: TRAVERSES EVERY EDGE EXACTLY ONCE. IN DIRECTED GRAPHS, IT EXISTS IF:

o ONE VERTEX HAS ONE EXTRA OUTGOING EDGE.
o ONE VERTEX HAS ONE EXTRA INCOMING EDGE.
o ALL OTHER VERTICES HAVE EQUAL INCOMING AND OUTGOING EDGES.
o EULERIAN CIRCUIT: A CLOSED PATH THAT TRAVERSES EVERY EDGE ONCE, POSSIBLE IF ALL VERTICES HAVE EQUAL INCOMING AND OUTGOING EDGES, AND THE GRAPH IS STRONGLY CONNECTED.
