KEY DIFFERENCES BETWEEN HAMILTONIAN AND EULERIAN GRAPHS:

• EULERIAN GRAPHS FOCUS ON VISITING EACH EDGE EXACTLY ONCE, WHEREAS HAMILTONIAN GRAPHS FOCUS ON VISITING EACH VERTEX EXACTLY ONCE.

• EULERIAN PATH/CIRCUIT: CONDITIONS FOR EXISTENCE ARE BASED ON THE DEGREE OF VERTICES.

• HAMILTONIAN PATH/CIRCUIT: CONDITIONS ARE MUCH MORE COMPLEX AND DEPEND ON THE GRAPH'S STRUCTURE RATHER THAN SIMPLE VERTEX DEGREE
